The Czech Republic , Poland and Hungary are the first former Warsaw Pact members that will become members of their one time opponent NATO . The Czech Republic is at the top of the list according to the Canadian Prime Minister . Russia is apparently uneasy about Czech membership and its ambassador to Prague has suggested it might reconsider its economic relations with the Czechs . But during a later visit the Russian Prime Minister Chernomyrdin said Moscow was enhancing economic ties with Prague , but opposes eastward expansion of NATO . A ceremony at the Harry Truman Library in Independence , Missouri marked the formal induction of these three countries .
