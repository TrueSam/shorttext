A 1989 NHTSA report claims the Ford Bronco II had the highest percentage of fatal roll-over accidents of vehicles in its class . The following year NHTSA reported that the Ford Club Wagon had the highest potential of all vehicles for driver head injury , while the Isuzu Trooper II had the highest potential for front-seat passenger injury . Also in 1990 , a Michigan association claimed the Chevrolet Corvette had the highest crash death rate of all vehicles , while the four-door Volvo had the lowest . Transportation Secretary Pena has said that 150 deaths occurred in otherwise survivable accidents involving GM trucks with side-mounted fuel tanks .
