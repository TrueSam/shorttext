In 1990 , vehicles with the lowest occupant death rate were the larger and heavier cars . Those with the highest occupant death rate were the cheapest cars and high performance vehicles . Also , in almost all vehicles , the person in the passenger seat was much more likely to sustain a head or chest injury than the driver , who was frequently protected by an airbag . Four-wheel drive vehicles had a high rollover rate , with the Ford Bronco II having the highest , while the Jeep had the lowest . General Motors ' trucks made between 1973 and 1987 with side-mounted fuel tanks caught fire in side-impact crashes .
