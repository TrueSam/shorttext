In December 1996 , India and Bangladesh signed a 30-year Ganges water-sharing agreement , resolving a major irritant in bilateral relations since 1974 when India built a dam near Farakka . Bangladesh had accused India of releasing excessive water during the monsoon season , while diverting much of the water during the dry season . The accord was made possible when India agreed to divorce the issue of rail transit through Bangladesh to the landlocked Indian state , Tripura . Other issues confronting the two nations include , drug smuggling , woman and child trafficking , disputed lands , border violence , and tariff concessions for Bangladesh to narrow the trade gap .
