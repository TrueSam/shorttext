A decrease in stratospheric ozone that naturally filters some of the sun 's ultraviolet ( UV ) rays will result in increased eye damage to unprotected people on earth . We might expect eye cancer , age-related cataracts , age-related macular degeneration , cancer of the circumocular skin , pterygium and photokeratitis . Children are particularly vulnerable and damage is cumulative over a lifetime . The best protection is UV blocking hats and sun glasses . People with xeroderma pigmentosum must avoid sunlight otherwise they run the risk of growths on the eyelids and cornea . Eye cancer patients are being treated fairly successfully with ' proton therapy ' and cataracts are treated very successfully with surgery .
