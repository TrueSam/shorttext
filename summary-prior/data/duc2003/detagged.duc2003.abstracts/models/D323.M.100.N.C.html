Plagiarism entails passing off words or ideas of other persons as your own , without attribution . Today cases abound , each seemingly unique , often controversial and sometimes with legal ramifications . In San Diego , staffers questioned their editors ' definition of plagiarism when it involved press releases . Jonathan Kendell , a former Wall Street Journal editor sued the paper for $ 12.64 million after he was fired for plagiarism , claiming the charge was false and his reputation ruined . The 1980 novel `` Wild Oats '' by Jacob Epstein contains highly distinctive passages that are almost identical to many in novelist Martin Amis ' well-praised `` The Rachel Papers , '' published in 1974 .
