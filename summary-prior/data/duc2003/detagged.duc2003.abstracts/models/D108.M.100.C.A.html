Eleven people contended for the Republican nomination for president in 2000 . By the time of the New Hampshire primary six candidates had dropped out and a seventh soon followed . McCain won New Hampshire , leaving the race to him , Bush , Forbes and Keyes . . Then Forbes dropped out . Bush won South Carolina , Iowa and Delaware while McCain claimed Michigan and Arizona . McCain faded in early March and Bush had such a commanding lead that McCain withdrew on March 10 , almost eight months before the election . The primaries proved anti-climactic but they pointed out problems with the system recognized at state and national level .
