Automobile recalls involving over 500,000 vehicles are not uncommon . In March 1989 , for example , the EPA said Ford would recall 513,000 cars that exceeded federal emission standards . Later that year , Ford announced another recall , nearly 1.3 million vehicles , again for violation of pollution control standards . In 1990 , GM recalled 1.7 million cars with sticking throttles , Ford recalled 1.4 million cars with defective seats , and Chrysler recalled 500,000 vehicles with rusty front suspension parts . A less typical recall is one by Mazda in 1990 . The company recalled less than three thousand sports cars because as many as three might have defective air bags .
