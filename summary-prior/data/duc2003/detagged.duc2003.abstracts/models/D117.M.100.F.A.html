The number of homeless in the U.S. is estimated as 500,000 to 600,000 . Many die young and many are murdered . Few cities are in a financial position to provide the basic needs : affordable housing , addiction and medical treatment , and job training . Some have used their police to forcibly move the homeless from place to place , including jail , thus shuffling the problem rather than solving it . Federal assistance is complicated by the difficulty of counting the homeless . The problem of the homeless is so complex that it may seem to defy solution , but one thing seems sure : affordable housing is the first step .
