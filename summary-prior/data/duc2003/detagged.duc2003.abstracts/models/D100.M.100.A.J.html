New anti-schizophrenia drugs inhibit the activity of dopamine , a neurotransmitter also linked to addictions . Anti-psychotic drugs are also being administered experimentally to people at risk for developing schizophrenia or display early signs , but critics of the prevention program charge that some people are being unnecessarily medicated . Some schizophrenics hear voices , and medication can n't always control this symptom . Once the gene that causes schizophrenia is identified , scientists hope that more efficacious drugs will emerge . Brain imaging , a technique used in the investigation of drugs to combat mental illness , developed from the pioneering research of Dr. Kety on cerebral blood flow .
