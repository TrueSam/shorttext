Most Americans support capital punishment but the margin has narrowed . A 1999 poll showed 71 % in favor , down from 80 % in 1994 . By 2000 it was 60 % of whom 31 % strongly supported and 29 % had doubts or reservations . In 1999 there were political reflections of growing opposition as measures reinstating the death penalty were defeated in Iowa , Maine , Massachusetts , and Michigan . A statement by the Roman Catholic bishops explained : `` The pace of executions is numbing . The discovery of people on death row who are innocent is frightening '' and capital punishment `` is often applied unfairly and in racially biased ways . ''
