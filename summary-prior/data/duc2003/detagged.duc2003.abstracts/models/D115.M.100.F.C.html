Census data is used to apportion the 435 Congressional House seats , distribute federal aid and reshape election districts . The 1990 census failed to count 8.4 million people and double counted 4.4 million people . The Clinton administration believed that accuracy would be improved by using statistical sampling methods , rather than a pure headcount . Republicans , fearful that Democrats would misuse sampling to increase their constituency , filed suit to prohibit its use . A lower court panel ruling that statistical sampling , though allowed , cannot be used for apportionment was upheld by the Supreme Court . Census then collected data under both methods , each to be used for different purposes .
