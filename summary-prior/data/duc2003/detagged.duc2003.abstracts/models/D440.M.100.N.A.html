Countries worldwide have laws against child labor , but enforcement is ineffective . A three-day enforcement campaign by the U.S. Department of Labor turned up 10,987 minors working illegally . Employers were fined . Abroad , violations are markedly more widespread . The 1990 Mexican census estimated that 418,575 12 to 14-year olds were in the work force . U.S. labor and consumer groups , UNICEF , and international labor organizations have called for boycotts of imports from such countries as China , India , Pakistan , Thailand and Mexico . The U.S. government has threatened violating nations with trade restrictions and banning of all goods made by children . Typically , as in China , stricter laws are passed but enforcement lags .
