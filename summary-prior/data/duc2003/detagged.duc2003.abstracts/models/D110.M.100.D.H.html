Massachusetts warned possible antitrust violations in ATM non-member surcharge agreements and obtained fee concessions . Banks refused extending concessions or designating surcharge-free ATMs . Connecticut 's two-year surcharge ban and Iowa 's eight-year ban , both through administrative action , were overruled by courts . The Pentagon proposed banning surcharges on military bases . Santa Monica 's City Council and San Francisco voters approved surcharge bans in 1999 . Other cities considered them . Banks sued and threatened to bar non-member ATM use and remove machines . Courts blocked the bans . Consumers reduced other-bank ATM use . Fee opponents have argued localities can regulate ATMs , banks are limited from withdrawing machines , banks already get reimbursement , surcharges merely increase bank earnings , and fees hurt small banks .
