`` Profiling '' by customs and immigration officials at border crossings is one thing , but `` profiling '' by police departments to discriminate against minorities is quite another . The Los Angeles Times has reported cases in which police appeared to act on the basis of anti-black/Latino prejudice rather than due cause . In the most flagrant case , the police violently assaulted a black driver after stopping him for a minor traffic offense . In another , an officer followed a Latino driver for several blocks , stopped next to him at a stop light , got a good look at him , and charged him with a violation . Traffic checkpoints facilitate profiling .
