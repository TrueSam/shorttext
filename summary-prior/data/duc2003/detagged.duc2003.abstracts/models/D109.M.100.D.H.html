A toxic strain of E-coli bacterium existing in 1 % of commercial cattle ( and possibly deer , sheep and goats ) can be passed to humans . E-coli in animal feces passes to meat surfaces during improper slaughtering . Eating undercooked contaminated hamburgers is a frequent illness source . Drinking well water contaminated by rainwater passing over manure , attending a festival in a cow pasture , and swimming in poorly chlorinated or unchlorinated water has produced illness . E-coli has spread through cured meat , unpasteurized fruit juice , unwashed ground crops , and unwashed hands following restroom use . Pasteurization and thorough cooking have n't protected sufficiently . E-coli is relatively acid-resistant . Its infectious dose is low . It mostly affects children and the elderly .
