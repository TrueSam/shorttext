It is estimated the 1990 census count missed 8.4 million Americans . Most of those were minorities , the poor , those living in urban areas . The democratic administration responsible for the 2000 census wanted to use statistical sampling , especially since those likely to be missed without sampling would likely vote democratic . The republicans opposed sampling , saying the constitution mandates a head count . A divided Supreme Court ruled against sampling , at least regarding numbers used in apportioning representatives among states . Democrats pushed for two counts . The sampled count would be used for allocating funds for federal programs , and aligning election districts . The republicans threatened to withhold census funds .
