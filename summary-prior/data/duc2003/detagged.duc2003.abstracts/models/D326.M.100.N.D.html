Between 1987 and 1994 there were four ferry disasters , each of which caused more than 100 deaths . In March 1987 a British ferry , the Herald of Free Enterprise , sank off the Belgian port of Zeebrugge with the loss of 193 lives . In April 1991 the Italian ferry , Moby Prince , ran into an oil tanker outside the port of Livorno . The death toll was 140 . The ferry Estonia , jointly owned by Estonia and Sweden , sank in September 1944 in rough seas 40 kilometers southwest of the Finnish Island of Uto . More than 900 died . More than 140 died when a Philippine ferry sank in November 1994 .
