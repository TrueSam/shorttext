In vitro fertilization ( IVF ) is a technique whereby the sperm and the egg are united in a laboratory , and the resultant embryo is then surgically implanted in the uterus . The embryo is implanted within 36 hours or frozen for later use In 1992 there were 192 IVF programs in the Unites States . Twenty years earlier there were two . The success rate for the IVF technique in the United States is 12 % . A Singapore hospital claims a viable pregnancy in 28 % of its procedures . Couples should not pursue further IVF if their first six attempts have not succeeded . IVF has led to controversies and bitter court battles .
