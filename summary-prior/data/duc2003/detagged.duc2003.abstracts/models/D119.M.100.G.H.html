Bitterness between India and Bangladesh impeded negotiations . India insisted Ganges water-sharing be linked to transit rights through Bangladesh , which Bangladesh refused to discuss before water-sharing resolution . New governments for both countries brightened prospects and the new PMs signed a water accord . India agreed to separate water-sharing from transit issues . Bangladesh proposed purchasing Indian electricity . Following Indian nuclear tests , Bangladeshi PM Hasina privately urged new Indian PM Vajpayee to show restraint . Subsequent ministerial-level talks discussed Bangladeshi refugees still in India since 1971 , a Vajpayee priority . Directors of Indian and Bangladeshi border security periodically discussed cooperation , but border violence increased . Bangladesh insisted India 's non-ratification of a 1974 treaty was the cause of disputes .
