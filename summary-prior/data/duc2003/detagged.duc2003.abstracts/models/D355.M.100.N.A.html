Marine remote-sensing techniques date from the mid-1960 's . The first integrated marine observation satellite , the SEASAT-A , was launched in 1978 . Improved versions of its detectors , including Synthetic Aperture Radar ( SAR ) , are operational on the ERS-1 satellite and are programmed for the Envosat-1 . SAR provides imagery under all light and weather conditions . The first ocean-color monitoring satellite , NIMBUS-7 , was launched in October 1978 and another is planned for 1994 . Marine remote sensing techniques not only provide a rich source for meteorological and oceanographic research , but have practical applications in ocean petroleum development , location of marine products , marine engineering , ocean exploration and detection of water contamination .
