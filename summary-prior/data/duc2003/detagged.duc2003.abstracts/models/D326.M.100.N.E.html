Three ferry sinkings in 1991 resulted in the loss of over 1180 lives . In April 1991 , the Italian ferry Moby Prince sank after colliding with an oil tanker , killing 140 people . In September , the ferry Estonia sank in the Baltic sea , killing 900 people following a probable failure or the front loading doors . Subsequently , a Philippine ferry sank , claiming more than 140 lives . The sinking of the Estonia raised new concerns about the safety of roll-on roll-off ferries . In 1987 , the British ferry Herald of Free Enterprise sank , killing 193 people , after a crew member failed to close the front loading doors .
