The summer of 1998 floods on the 3,900-mile long Yangtze River in China reached historic proportions . Three thousand were killed and the cost was estimated at $ 31.7 billion . The flood control work focused on building and reinforcing dikes and dams , evacuating residents , and intentionally flooding selected areas to protect other areas . The Chinese realized other control measures must be addressed . Logging , grazing , and fuel gathering had denuded hills up-stream . Humans had encroached on wetlands , robbing their ability to absorb water . Lake storage declined because of silt build-up and factory construction . In 1999 money was earmarked for dredging and resettlement from wetlands .
