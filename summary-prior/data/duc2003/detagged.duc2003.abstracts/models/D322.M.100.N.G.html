Beginning in September 1989 , local and federal authorities began the largest confiscations ever of suspected fraudulent classical and modern art . Arrested in a series of raids were co-conspirators art broker Frank De Marigny ; former Upstairs Gallery manager , Lee Sonnier ; and prints wholesaler Larry Groeger . In June 1990 , continuing investigations led to the arrest of Upstairs Gallery president , William McKelvey , art forger Anthony Tetro , and art dealer Mark Sawicki . In April 1989 , San Diego art dealer , Luis Esquivel Almeida , was arrested for selling forged works of Olaf Weighorst . In Canada in April 1990 , Gregg Gailbraithe pleded guilty to peddling forgeries of famous maritime artists .
