Institutions are trying to stop or contain tribal violence in Africa . Former Tanzanian President Nyerere created a foundation in 1996 to work beyond tribal differences . The U.S. wanted an `` African Crisis Response Force '' to quell tribal warring . The U.N. wanted a 1,400-man peace keeping force formed to pacify the Central African Republic . The U.S. sent Jesse Jackson as a special envoy to Kenya where the Kikuyu and Kalenjin were battling . He urged President Moi to pursue reconciliation . The South African Truth and Reconciliation Commission , which had investigated mainly interracial violence , was urged to examine black on black violence , which had killed 30,000 people .
