About 240 million tires are discarded in the United States each year . A small percentage of these tires can be retreaded for future use and various initiatives are underway to recycle those remaining . Uses for discarded tires include grinding them up into a `` crumb . '' The crumb is used to manufacture carpet underlay as well as being mixed with asphalt for paving . Paving a mile with a rubber-asphalt mix can use the equivalent of 16,000 tires . Discarded tires also are being made into jogging tracks , crash barriers , jungle gyms , mud flaps , sandals , doormats and hockey pucks . Discarded tires also are tied together to make artificial reefs .
