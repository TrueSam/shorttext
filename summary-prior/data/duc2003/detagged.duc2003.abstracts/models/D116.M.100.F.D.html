The dispute over teaching evolution in Kansas schools started in 1998 when a committee was appointed to recommend new science standards . The School Board , spurred by conservatives , cast aside the committee 's recommendations and approved a standard that barely mentioned evolution . The new standard made no mention of macroevolution , defined as changes from one species to another . The 304 local school districts were left to implement the standard . The Kansas decision drew nationwide attention . National science organizations criticized it . Educators said that Kansas college-bound students would be poorly prepared in the sciences . Kansas received an F-minus in a nationwide rating of how states taught evolution .
