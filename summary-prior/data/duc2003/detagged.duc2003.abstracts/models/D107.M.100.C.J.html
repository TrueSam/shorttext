An August 2000 poll of registered voters indicates most Americans support the death penalty , but many with reservations . The concern most often voiced was that an innocent person would be executed . A poll taken in California in early 2000 revealed that nearly half of respondents thought that life without parole , rather than the death penalty , was the proper punishment for murder . There is also concern among lawmakers and the public that the application of the death penalty is racially and economically discriminatory . Finally , an interfaith conference of religious leaders called the death penalty `` morally untenable . ''
