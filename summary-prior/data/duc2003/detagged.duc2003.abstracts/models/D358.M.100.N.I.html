The blood-alcohol level played a key role in 17 accidents reported in the Los Angles Times during 1989 and 1990 . The 17 drivers were tested with levels between .10 and .29 percent , three times the legal limit . This limit was lowered from .10 to .08 percent in 1990 . Twenty-nine people , including only one driver , were killed in these accidents . The drunken drivers were both men and women and came from as diverse careers as a county prosecutor and a professional athlete . One of these drivers caused the accident that killed former baseball major-league manager Billy Martin .
