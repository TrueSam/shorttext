Flooding in Mozambique in early 2000 killed at least 492 people . One million people were left either homeless or in desperate need of help . The floods caused an estimated $ 250 million in damage to roads , bridges , railways , and buildings . Vast areas of agricultural land were destroyed , leading to acute hunger . In some cases , water and farmlands were contaminated . Many people needed medical attention . Landmines from the two-decade civil war , which ended in 1992 , were displaced ; large-scale de-mining and the rehabilitation of victims of war were expected to slow . Mozambique 's growth rate , roughly 10 percent a year , was expected to fall because of the disaster .
