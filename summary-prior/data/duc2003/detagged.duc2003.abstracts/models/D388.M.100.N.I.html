Western nations were using sewer sludge for organic farming . Various approaches included tilling it directly into the soil , composting it with wood and household waste , freeze-drying , and liquefying and injecting into the soil . To avoid contaminating food , this sludge was applied to industrial crops , grain , and grasses . In Russia , farmyard manure and compost were used for potato and other vegetable crops . In Cuba , a mixture of ash , vegetable matter , and microorganisms was applied to rice , sugarcane , and other crops . Pelleted chicken feather manure and seaweed extract were tested on wheat . Redwood compost and composted newspaper and grass were popular for home gardening .
