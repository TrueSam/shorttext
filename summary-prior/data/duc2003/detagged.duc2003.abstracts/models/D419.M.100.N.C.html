Promising new methods are being explored worldwide for tire recycling . In the U.S. alone , about 240 million old tires are discarded each year . One initiative is to grind up tires into rubber `` crumb '' which is then incorporated into other products such as road asphalt , rug underlay , drain pipes and insulation panels . A second is to fuel energy plants by burning treated tires in an environmentally acceptable manner . And a third is to chemically break tires down into by-products , including fuel gas , oil , zinc and carbon black . The carbon can be used as an absorbent of organic chemicals from industrial effluent .
