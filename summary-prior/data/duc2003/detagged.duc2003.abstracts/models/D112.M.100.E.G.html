Two years after astronauts replaced and repaired older scientific instruments in the Hubble Space Telescope in 1997 , failing gyroscopes forced NASA to schedule its regular repair mission about eight months early . The plan is to replace all six gyroscopes and essential computer , radio , camera , guiding systems and solar panels . Just before the scheduled launch in November , a fourth gyroscope faltered forcing celestial observations to halt . A shuttle , launched in early December , captured the Hubble and the crew replaced the six gyroscopes on the first spacewalk . On the second spacewalk , astronauts replaced the central computer and a precision pointing sensor device .
