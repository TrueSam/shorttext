E. Coli outbreaks vary in extent , virulence and the nature of the carrier . Since first recognized as a major health hazard in 1993 a strain of E. Coli has appeared repeatedly and may cause 20,000 illnesses and 250-500 deaths per year in the U.S. Worldwide outbreaks include one in Japan in 1996 that affected 9,200 individuals and caused seven deaths . While usually blamed on improper processing or handling of foodstuffs or drinking of polluted water , a few outbreaks appeared to be associated with swimming in chlorinated water . Whatever the carrier the source of the bacteria is ultimately probably cow manure or human feces .
