Recent increases in death penalty opposition have been prompted by discoveries of innocent people on death row , requests for clemency by public figures , and pleas for death penalty renunciation by the UN , the ABA and the Pope . Concerns have arisen over shortened appeals times , numbers of condemned , judicial system errors , and death penalty expansions . Opponents have become more effective . Some opposition is moral , stressing the sanctity of life and need to show compassion and forgiveness . Others claim the death penalty is unfairly applied to the poor and minorities . Others say capital punishment continues the cycle of violence without providing healing , closure , or crime deterrence . Life sentences without parole are sufficient .
