The European Union proposed that a single currency , the euro , come into use in January 1999 . Eleven members of the 15-member European Union were accepted as founding countries of the single currency . Britain and Denmark opted out of the first wave . Greece and Sweden were not economically ready . From the beginning Britain had expressed reservations , citing potential harm to British businesses . France , Germany and Italy were solidly in favor of the euro . Italy looked upon it as a step toward European integration . In January 1999 the euro was used in bank and stock market written exchanges . Asian and U.S. traders welcomed it .
