The organizers of the Salt Lake City Olympic Games were accused of buying the votes of IOC members three years after winning the right to host the 2002 Winter Games . They spent $ 800,000 on 14 IOC members . They said that `` humanitarian aid '' provided to some countries was not vote-buying . Others disagreed . Cities that lost in the bidding said they had been cheated . Thirteen individuals , six of them relatives of IOC members , received $ 400,000 scholarship money . Another $ 60,000 was spent entertaining an influential Australian official . A Chilean politician received $ 10,000 . Agents played a role in arranging deals . Two Salt Lake City organizers were indicted .
