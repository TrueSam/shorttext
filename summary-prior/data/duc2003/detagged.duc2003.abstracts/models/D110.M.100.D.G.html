Banks customers are beginning to resist double charges on ATM use at federally chartered banks . The problem centers around the fee charged to the banks ' customer for using other banks ' ATM , and additional fees for non-customers use of the banks ' ATM . State and local governments are increasingly promoting bans on these double charges on the grounds that they are gouging customers . Federal courts tend to support bank claims that federally chartered banks are not subject to local and state laws . Monthly surcharges at two San Francisco banks collectively totaled $ 455,000 . Banks say the charges are necessary to pay for growing ATM networks .
