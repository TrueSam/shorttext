Banks are using every tactic to fight what was a popular consumer movement to stop double charges for using another bank 's ATM . Consumer advocates estimate the charge exacted by the ATM owner generates more than $ 2 billion in revenue for banks nationwide each year . This charge was banned in Connecticut and Iowa , and eventually two California cities . In New England , banks offered to limit charges in exchange for permission to merge . Banks fought in the courts saying they answered only to federal laws . They cut-off , or threatened to cut-off , non-customer users . National-level government actions were talked about but never enacted .
