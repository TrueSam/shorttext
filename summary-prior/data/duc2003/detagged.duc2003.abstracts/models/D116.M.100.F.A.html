In August 1999 the Kansas Board of Education voted 6-4 against new standards on science drawn up by a committee of scientists , removing all mention of the big bang theory and the origin of the species . Opponents thought the new standards hinted at teaching creationism in public schools , ruled unconstitutional by the Supreme Court . In September 2000 a report to the American Association for the Advancement of Science gave grades to the 50 states on the teaching of evolution and Kansas was lowest with an `` F- '' -- even below Tennessee where Scopes was found guilty of teaching evolution 75 years before .
