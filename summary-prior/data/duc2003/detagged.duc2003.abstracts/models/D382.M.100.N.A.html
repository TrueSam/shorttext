There has been progress in development of automobiles powered by hydrogen -- either through a fuel cell which produces electricity or as a fuel for rotary engines . Daimler-Benz has produced a fuel cell vehicle and predicts by 1996 production of a fuel cell which can fit into a passenger car . The problem with fuel cells is their limited life span . Mazda 's HR-X2 concept car has a rotary engine fueled by a hydrogen gas stored in a fuel tank . The problem is how to fit enough hydrogen into a tank and ultimately how to produce enough hydrogen to provide a significant substitution for petroleum .
