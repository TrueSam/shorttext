Canada divided its Northwest Territory into two , giving the Intuit people a territory of their own , renamed Nunavut . For over twenty years they negotiated their aboriginal land claim with the Canadian government . Agreeing to give up all future claims , their settlement culminated with the new territory , outright title to 135,000 square miles of the territory , significant mineral rights and $ 1 billion over 14 years . Isolated , the Inuit people have been able to maintain their language and culture . Although excited about self-governance , old problems persist . Nunavut remains the poorest of Canada 's territories and the Intuit have severe problems with alcoholism , suicide and crime .
