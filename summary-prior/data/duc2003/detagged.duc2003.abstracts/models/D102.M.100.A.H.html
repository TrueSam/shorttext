Charles M. Schulz 's childhood ambition was to produce a comic strip . He took a cartooning correspondence course and created a comic starring Charlie Brown that appeared nationally in 1950 . It continued uninterrupted and became the most popular strip in the world . Schulz characters were aspects of him . Charlie Brown had a barber father like Schulz and Snoopy a colorful interior life . Peanuts spin-offs made Schulz rich . He stopped drawing the strip at 77 after cancer diagnosis and died February 12th , 2000 , the day before the last strip . When he received a lifetime achievement award , other strips included Peanuts references . Groundbreaking for a Peanuts museum Schulz had planned began in June .
