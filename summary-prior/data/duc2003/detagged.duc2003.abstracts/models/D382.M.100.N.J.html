Mazda and Daimler-Benz are pursuing different strategies for developing a commercially viable hydrogen fueled automobile . Mazda , in 1991 , demonstrated a concept car that used hydrogen gas stored in fuel tanks containing a hydrogen-occluded alloy . The gas is directly injected into the proprietary rotary engine cylinders immediately after completion of the intake stoke , resulting in a higher output than that produced during earlier experiments when both air and hydrogen were injected simultaneously . Daimler-Benz is experimenting with fuel cells that convert hydrogen ( obtained from methanol ) to electricity by electrochemical means . Daihatsu Motor Co. and Honda have also demonstrated hydrogen fueled vehicles .
