Organic fertilizers help soil retain moisture . Cuba uses a mix of ash , vegetable waste and microorganisms . England 's Kew gardeners work manure and compost into their beds . And in Russia , farmyard manure and compost are favored . But the coming thing in soil enhancers is sewage sludge . California uses wet sludge , with or without yard clippings , for crops not intended for human consumption . The U.K. also uses sludge for cereal crops and grasslands . A French company dries sludge and mixes in bark , sawdust and household waste . While some activists argue that sewage sludge may pose a health hazard , it is clearly spurting forth as an organic fertilizer .
