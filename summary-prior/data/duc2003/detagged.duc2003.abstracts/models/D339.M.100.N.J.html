Nootropics is a new class of drugs to enhance cognition . Early nootropics increased the amount of acetylcholine , a neurotransmitter found at markedly low levels in Alzheimer patients . Oxiracetam ( Smith Kline Beecham ) , physotigmine ( Forest Laboratories ) , and tacrine ( Cognex ) from Warner-Lambert , inhibit an enzyme which attacks acetylcholine , but all of these drugs offer only temporary relief from the disease 's symptoms . According to Dr. Sykes , ondansetron is safer and as effective as Cognex , the first FDA approved drug specifically for the treatment of Alzheimer 's . It appears that the narrow therapeutic index of some early acetylcholinesterase inhibitors limits their administration at adequate doses to affect cognition .
